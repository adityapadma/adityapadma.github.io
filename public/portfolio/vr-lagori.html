VR Lagori

The Nostalgic Stack

Reimagining a traditional childhood game as a poetic, interactive memory space where rules guide emotion.

[ Hero Image: A glowing stack of stones in a void ]

1. Core Goal

To evoke childhood nostalgia through embodied play.

Actions like walking, picking up stones, stacking, and throwing become physical triggers for memory. The experience does not simply simulate a game; it reimagines it as a poetic, interactive memory space where rules guide emotion.

Concept Memory trigger
Mechanism Physical Action

Design Intent

"The experience does not simply simulate a game; it reimagines it as a poetic, interactive memory space."

2. Why Extended Reality?

Embodiment

Engaging the player's body directly—standing, reaching, and bending—to activate muscle memory.

Spatial Presence

Physically occupying the human-scale childhood environment rather than observing it on a screen.

Multisensory

Spatial audio of children laughing and haptic "memory activation" during stone interaction.

Key Principles

  • Somatic Engagement
  • Human scale
  • Haptic feedback

3. Interaction & Materiality

01

Movement

Natural locomotion. Slow-paced walking encourages reflection over speed.

02

Gesture

Reaching, grabbing, and rotating runic stones using VR controllers with precision alignment.

03

Gaze

No explicit UI. Attention is guided by glowing paths and "hand palm" stone counters.

04

Body

The act of standing up initiates the experience, reinforcing bodily agency from the start.

"Stones appear worn and uneven. Each phase triggers a different quality of physical interaction, building emotional pacing through bodily engagement."

Gamification Layer

Clear goals, stone collection rules, and rule-based interaction guide the player loop.

Artification Layer

Atmospheric lighting, "memory echoes," and slow pacing transform play into a reflective encounter.

6. Experience Outcomes

Somatic Memory

Physical actions like bending and stacking activate long-dormant autobiographical memories.

Intrinsic Reward

No scores or points. Progression and the emotional payoff of the final hit are the rewards.

Spatial Art

Moving away from literal simulation toward a spatial art installation grounded in play.

Memory through Action

VR Lagori preserves the invisible heritage of play by making the user physically perform the heritage.
