1. ARTS: Speculative Urban Design

IIIT Delhi • Creative Interfaces Lab

"How can the AR Tabletop/Sandbox function as a Speculative Design Tool for citizen participation in urban design?"

ARTS (AR Tabletop/Sandbox) is a speculative system designed to bridge the gap between expert urban planning tools and citizen imagination. By combining a physical sandtable with "Future Cards" as tangible tokens, we allow citizens to visualize scenarios for parks, green spaces, and third spaces via a GenAI pipeline.

Future Cards

Tangible tokens used to prompt speculative urban futures.

GenAI Pipeline

Stable Diffusion-powered visualization of citizen built scenarios.

The Technical implementation

Implemented in Python for token detection (YOLOv8 + ArUco) and Unity for the digital twin representation. The final visuals are handled by custom ComfyUI pipelines, ensuring stable representations of urban density without hallucinating the number of tokens placed.

2. Sculpting Intelligence

Real-Time AI Dialogue

While traditional generative interfaces often lack fluid feedback, Sculpting Intelligence enables a real-time dialogue between user and AI. Our pipeline couples direct physical sand manipulation with the near-instantaneous visual feedback of the StreamDiffusion model.

A Kinect sensor interprets the sand's topography, which our TouchDesigner pipeline converts into dynamic heatmaps representing elevation and slope. These heatmaps guide the AI to generate biomes such as mountains, deserts, and oceans that correspond directly to the sand's form.

3. Pipeline & Future Work

1

Physical Capture

Kinect V2 captures Depth, Color, and IR feeds. OpenCV identifies ArUco markers and hand gestures via MediaPipe.

2

Digital Mapping

Python normalizes 2D coordinates and streams them to Unity via TCP/JSON. Unity aligns the digital twin with the physical topography.

3

Generative Synthesis

Dynamic heatmaps are paired with prompts in ComfyUI or StreamDiffusion. Latent diffusion processes generate high-fidelity speculative renders.

4

Projection Feedback

The generated image is scaled to 1920x1080 and projected back onto the sandtable, closing the physical-digital loop.
