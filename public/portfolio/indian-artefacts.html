Design Research Case Study

Playing, Watching, Telling.

Reinterpreting Indian Vernacular Cultures and Artefacts through Digital New Media.

The Problem Space

Indian vernacular artefacts are not dying because they are undocumented—they are dying because they are no longer experienced.

A 3D scan cannot capture the soul of the Rajasthani Kaavad, a wooden shrine whose meaning lives in the performer's voice and the physical act of unfolding its panels. Similarly, uploading street Bioscope films to YouTube strips away the shared, collaborative gaze of the audience.

This project addresses a critical shift from documentation to experience. These artefacts are not just objects; they are embodied rituals, gestures, and social contracts. Preserving them is an experiential problem, not an archival one.

The Design Philosophy

Three core patterns emerged for translating traditional artefacts into the digital realm.

01

The Ritual Gesture as Interface

Moving from 'displaying' heritage to 'performing' it. The interaction itself must mirror the cultural act—whether it's pulling a root to branch a narrative or stacking stones. The gesture precedes the content in significance.

02

Affective Fidelity over Visual

Why the feeling matters more than the 3D mesh. We prioritize preserving the emotional and somatic memory of the artefact—the anticipation, the weight, the effort—rather than creating hyper-realistic visual simulations.

03

The Social Contract

Artefacts dictate how we interact with each other. A Bioscope requires shared focus; a Kaavad requires a narrator and a patron. Designing for heritage means designing for the collective gaze and shared memory.

The Projects

Tangible interactive installation

Completed

KaavadBits

Narrative Branching through Tangible Play

A reinterpretation of the 400-year-old Rajasthani Kaavad Baanchana storytelling tradition. Instead of recreating the wooden box, KaavadBits embodies the narrator-patron perspective through a diegetic tree.

  • ✦ Users pull physical hanging roots to make binary narrative choices in Panchatantra fables.
  • ✦ Interaction involves placing NFC-enabled character figurines on an oscillating platform.
  • ✦ Validated through a pilot study achieving an above-average System Usability Scale (SUS) score of 78.75.

Immersive VR somatic experience

In Development

VR Lagori: The Nostalgic Stack

Activating Somatic Motor Memory

A poetic reinterpretation of the traditional seven-stone street game. Designed not as a literal simulation, but as a memory space for young adults, relying heavily on the physical weight and spatial presence of VR.

  • ✦ Uses VR hand controllers to invoke the full-body engagement of bending, reaching, precision stacking, and throwing.
  • ✦ Utilizes 'memory echoes'—abstract sound layers, light ripples, and ghost-like streaks—rather than literal flashbacks.
  • ✦ Emotional payoff relies entirely on intrinsic physical action, devoid of points or scoring systems.

Hybrid mechanical-VR artefact

Proposed

VR Bioscope

Restoring the Collaborative Gaze

The essence of the Indian street Bioscope was the shared crouch, the anticipation of the manual crank, and looking together. This hybrid artefact replaces peepholes with VR headsets while retaining the mechanical physical crank.

  • ✦ The physical crank acts as the primary embodied input, controlling the pace and resistance of the VR animation.
  • ✦ Innovation: Animation is triggered only through the collaborative gaze of 2-3 simultaneous users looking at the same point.
  • ✦ Transforms the act of solitary watching back into a collective performative ritual.

Nostalgia as an Interface

This research reframes nostalgia—not as a surface aesthetic, but as a primary interaction modality. By using the body’s memory of a cultural gesture as the entry point, we bridge the gap between traditional heritage and contemporary urban audiences.

Interaction design is a necessary partner to archival practice. To keep vernacular culture alive, we must design tools that allow us not just to look at the past, but to continuously perform it.
